# Ethics & Responsible Use

## Status

Auracelle Charlie is a doctoral research instrument under active development at Bath Spa University. Its use in human participant research is subject to institutional ethical review.

## Research ethics

**Participant consent**
When running facilitated sessions with human participants, researchers must:
- Obtain informed consent before the session, including purpose, data use, and right to withdraw
- Explain that session data (turn logs, deliverables, rationale text) will be retained for research analysis
- Not record names or identifying information in free-text fields unless explicitly consented to
- Follow institutional IRB/ethics board requirements for the specific research context

**Data minimisation**
- Record only what is needed for research objectives
- Do not enter sensitive personal, classified, or operationally sensitive information into any field
- Use session IDs rather than participant names in research records

**Demographic composition groups**
The instrument includes All-Female and All-Male composition groups as research variables under Objective 3. These groups are designed for gender-differentiated governance research. Facilitators should be sensitive to participant comfort with group assignment and ensure participation is voluntary.

## AI and agentic features

**Policy Owner and Red Team agents (Tab ⑩)**
These features use the Anthropic API (Claude Sonnet 4). When active:
- Session state (scenario, round, governance indicators, turn log excerpts, and deliverable text) is transmitted to Anthropic's API
- Do not include personally identifying participant information in turn rationale fields if using these features
- API usage is subject to Anthropic's terms of service and privacy policy
- The API key is stored in browser session memory only and is not logged by this application

**Autonomous Simulation (Tab ⑧)**
The autonomous simulation generates synthetic governance session data for research comparison. Its outputs are not predictions of real-world governance outcomes and should not be interpreted as such.

## Limitations

- Session indicator values are derived from structured turn data using simplified aggregation functions. They are research operationalisations, not validated psychometric instruments.
- Foresight validation comparisons against historical cases are illustrative. They do not constitute formal predictive validation without additional methodological steps.
- MARL and autonomous simulation outputs are algorithmically generated and subject to stochasticity. Treat them as comparison data, not ground truth.

## Prohibited uses

- Do not use this instrument to make operational, legal, or binding policy decisions
- Do not represent simulation outputs as authoritative policy analysis in professional or governmental contexts without appropriate caveats
- Do not use participant data collected via this instrument for purposes other than those consented to
